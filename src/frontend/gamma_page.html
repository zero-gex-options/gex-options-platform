Max Gamma Strike ℹ️ Maximum Gamma Strike

The strike with the highest total gamma acts as a "price magnet" - especially near expiration. Price tends to gravitate toward this level as dealers hedge.

Above Max Gamma: Slight bearish pull
Below Max Gamma: Slight bullish pull

Gamma Flip Point ℹ️ Zero-Gamma Level

The price where net GEX crosses zero. Critical inflection point:

Above Flip: Positive gamma regime (stabilizing). Bullish.
Below Flip: Negative gamma regime (destabilizing). Bearish.

Max Pain ℹ️ Max Pain Strike

The strike price where the most option value would expire worthless at expiration. This represents the price that would cause maximum losses for option buyers.

Often acts as a magnet near expiration as market makers may hedge towards this level.

Put/Call Ratio ℹ️ Put/Call Open Interest Ratio

Ratio of put to call open interest.

High (>1.0): More puts than calls - can indicate hedging/fear. Bearish tilt.
Low (<0.7): More calls than puts - bullish positioning. Bullish tilt.
~0.7-1.0: Balanced positioning.
